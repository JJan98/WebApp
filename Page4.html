About Us

The sixth generation Mustang was unveiled on December 5, 2013, in Dearborn, Michigan, New York, New York, Los Angeles, California, Barcelona, Spain, Shanghai, China, and Sydney, Australia. The internal project code name is S-550.

Changes include a widened body by 1.5 inches, a lower body height of 1.4 inches, a trapezoidal grille, and a 2.75-inch lower decklid, along with new color options. The passenger volume has been increased to 84.5 cubic feet, while the wheelbase remains at 8 ft. 11.1 in. (107.1 inches). There are three engine options available: a newly developed 2.3 L EcoBoost 310 hp four-cylinder, introduced to target high-tariff global markets like China, a 3.7 L 300 hp V6, and a 5.0 L Coyote 435 hp V8. These engines are paired with either a Getrag six-speed manual or a six-speed automatic transmission with paddle shifters.

A new independent rear suspension (IRS) system was developed specifically for the new model. It also became the first version designed for factory right-hand drive, intended for export to be sold through Ford's new car dealerships in right-hand drive markets. During this model year, left-hand drive versions were expanded to new export markets.
